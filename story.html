Our Story

Priyani Lace began in quiet evenings, lit by candlelight. It’s a story handed down from mother to daughter in a small village in Southern Sri Lanka, where delicate lace-making was slowly fading away. With wooden bobbins, patience, and tradition, Priyani revived this beautiful craft, weaving not just cloth but continuity.

Over the years, this humble art has become a lifeline for women: cancer survivors, widows, elderly artisans, and young village girls. Every stitch tells a story of resilience, love, and pride — binding a community together.

Today, every piece of Priyani Lace carries a living legacy. It’s not mass-produced but lovingly handcrafted — slow, personal, and powerful. When you wear Priyani Lace, you wear a story that lives beyond the fabric.
